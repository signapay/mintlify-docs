title: "Boarding a Partner"
description: "Step-by-step guide to onboarding a new ISO or referral partner onto PayHub"
---

This guide walks through the full partner onboarding lifecycle — from creating the partner record through business profile setup, pricing configuration, and final activation. Each step links to the relevant API endpoint or overview for details.

## Prerequisites

Before you start, you need an authenticated session or API key with sufficient privileges to manage partner records. See the [Authentication Overview](/tutorials/authentication/overview) for supported schemes.

- **Session token** — Obtain one via [Create Session](/api-reference/authentication/create-session). Pass it as `Bearer <token>` in the `Authorization` header.
- **API key** — Long-lived keys for service-to-service integrations. Managed via [Client API Keys](/api-reference/clients/get-client-api-key).

---

## Boarding steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the partner record">
    A partner record is the root entity for an ISO or referral organization. All subsequent configuration — addresses, principals, pricing, and documents — attaches to this record.

    **Endpoint:** `POST /v1/crm/partners`

    Provide the partner's legal name, DBA name, and the client it belongs to. Refer to the [Partners Overview](/api-reference/partners/overview) for a full description of available fields and the partner hierarchy.

    ```http
    POST /v1/crm/partners
    Authorization: Bearer <token>
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a business address">
    Attach the partner's physical business location to the record.

    **Endpoint:** [Add Partner Address](/api-reference/partners/add-partner-address) — `POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/business-addresses`

    Include street, city, state, and ZIP. Multiple addresses can be added if the partner operates from more than one location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add principals">
    Capture beneficial owners, key contacts, and signers for the partner organization.

    **Endpoint:** [Add Partner Principal](/api-reference/partners/add-partner-principal) — `POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/principals`

    Sensitive fields such as SSN and date of birth are encrypted at rest. Use [Decrypt Partner Principal](/api-reference/partners/decrypt-partner-principal) to retrieve protected values when needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach supporting documents">
    Upload any required compliance or contractual documents for the partner.

    **Endpoint:** [Create Partner Document](/api-reference/partners/create-partner-document) — `POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/documents`

    Documents are stored against the partner record and can be retrieved individually or listed via [List Partner Documents](/api-reference/partners/list-partner-documents).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure pricing">
    Set up the fee structure that defines the partner's cost schedule and the pricing structures it can apply to downstream merchants.

    - **Pricing items** — [Create Pricing Item](/api-reference/partners/create-partner-pricing-item) (`POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/pricing-items`). Individual fee line items such as interchange pass-through, basis points, and per-transaction fees.
    - **Pricing packages** — [Create Pricing Package](/api-reference/partners/create-partner-pricing-package) (`POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/pricing-packages`). Group related pricing items into a named configuration.
    - **Pricing templates** — [Create Pricing Template](/api-reference/partners/create-partner-pricing-template) (`POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/pricing-templates`). Reusable pricing structures the partner can apply when boarding its own merchants.
    - **Pricing rules** — [Create Pricing Rule](/api-reference/partners/create-partner-pricing-rule) (`POST /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/pricing-rules`). Apply conditional pricing logic, such as volume tiers or card-type overrides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review processor settings">
    Confirm or update the processor-specific configuration for the partner. These settings control how the partner's downstream transactions are routed and processed.

    - **List settings** — [List Partner Processor Settings](/api-reference/partners/list-partner-processor-settings) (`GET /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/processor-settings`). Review all processor configuration entries.
    - **Update a setting** — [Update Partner Processor Setting](/api-reference/partners/update-partner-processor-setting) (`PUT /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/processor-settings/{processorSettingId}`). Modify an individual entry as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate the partner">
    Once all configuration is in place, transition the partner to an active status so it can begin boarding merchants and agents.

    **Endpoint:** [Update Partner Status](/api-reference/partners/update-partner-status) — `PUT /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/{action}`

    Pass `activate` as the `action` path parameter. See the [Partners Overview](/api-reference/partners/overview) for a description of all available status transitions.

    ```http
    PUT /v1/crm/partners/{partnerId}/activate
    Authorization: Bearer <token>
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>
